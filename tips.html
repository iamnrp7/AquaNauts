Water Saving Tips

Rain Barrel System

Harvest rainwater for outdoor use by creating a rain barrel system.

Materials Required for Rain Barrel System:

  • A large plastic barrel or container
  • Gutter system and downspout
  • Screen or filter to prevent debris
  • Overflow hose
  • Spigot or faucet
  • Sealant and tools for installation

Installation Steps:

  1. Place the rain barrel beneath a downspout from your roof.
  2. Install a screen or filter over the top of the barrel to prevent debris from entering.
  3. Connect the downspout to the barrel's inlet using a gutter system.
  4. Install an overflow hose to divert excess water away from your foundation.
  5. Drill a hole near the bottom of the barrel and attach a spigot or faucet.
  6. Seal all connections to prevent leaks.
  7. Collect and use rainwater for your outdoor needs.

Low-Flow Faucets

Install low-flow faucets to reduce water usage in your home.

Materials Required for Low-Flow Faucet Installation:

  • Low-flow faucet fixtures
  • Adjustable wrench
  • Teflon tape (plumber's tape)
  • Plumber's putty (optional)

Installation Steps:

  1. Turn off the water supply to the faucet.
  2. Remove the existing faucet by using an adjustable wrench.
  3. Clean the sink surface and remove any old putty or debris (if applicable).
  4. Apply plumber's tape to the threads of the new low-flow faucet fixtures.
  5. Insert the new faucet into the sink or countertop hole and tighten it with an adjustable wrench.
  6. Turn the water supply back on and check for leaks.
  7. Your low-flow faucet is now installed, reducing water usage in your home.
